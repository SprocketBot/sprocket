# Sprocket Level 5 Spec: Executive Charter

Sprocket exists to make competitive Rocket League operations trustworthy, fast, and scalable.

Its job is not merely to host workflows. Its job is to turn gameplay evidence, player coordination, and league policy into official outcomes that the community can trust.

## Mission

Provide a league operations platform that:
- turns replay evidence into enforceable results,
- reduces routine staff intervention,
- keeps players, staff, and community channels synchronized around the same source of truth.

## What Must Be True

These commitments define the system more than any specific implementation:
- Official outcomes must be backed by evidence and policy, not informal reporting.
- Users must be told the current state and next action with minimal ambiguity.
- Discord and web must behave like coordinated surfaces of one operational system.
- Organization-specific policy must be configurable without fragmenting the platform.
- Legacy compatibility may support the platform, but it must not define its future.

## Strategic Standard

Sprocket is successful when competition administration becomes mostly exception-driven:
- routine workflows advance automatically,
- disputes narrow to explicit evidence and rule interpretation,
- moderators spend time on judgment calls rather than clerical work.

## Decision Test

Major changes should generally be accepted if they improve one or more of the following without weakening trust:
- evidence integrity,
- policy clarity,
- operator leverage,
- user clarity,
- platform evolvability.

Changes should generally be rejected if they trade correctness for convenience, duplicate policy in multiple places, or make authoritative state harder for users and staff to understand.

## End-State Vision

The desired end state is a platform where competitive policy is explicit, evidence pipelines are deterministic, operational communication is real time, and community tooling feels native instead of bolted on.

## Relationship to Earlier Levels

- Level 1 inventories the code surface.
- Level 2 explains subsystem behavior.
- Level 3 defines architecture intent and invariants.
- Level 4 defines strategy and investment direction.
- Level 5 (this document) states the shortest-form charter for what Sprocket is and how to judge its evolution.
